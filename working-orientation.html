Working Orientation

These notes help me show up consistently and responsively in life, especially when situations feel unclear or uncomfortable.

They are not ideals or promises. I keep them as functional reminders for action when my attention drifts toward self-evaluation instead of what’s actually needed.

I’m aiming less for “confidence” and more for availability — staying present, responding honestly, and repairing quickly when needed.

  • Stay closer to what’s happening than to the story about it. Attention over narration.
  • Act without requiring comfort, certainty, or approval. Recoverability matters more than confidence.
  • Be useful where possible; don’t disappear to be helpful. Contribution without erasure.
  • Speak what’s true enough for the moment. Clarity over performance.
  • Trust myself to respond and repair. No pre-negotiation with fear.

I tend to return to this when I feel reactive, overly self-conscious, or stuck in narrative rather than response.

Re-read when stuck. Ignore when things are flowing.
