FIXED LOAD TRAINING — STANDARD PURPOSE Reach a defined level of strength early. Maintain it unchanged for life. ENTRY STANDARD ≈ 300 Wilks EQUIPMENT - Barbell - 25 lb plates (one pair) - 45 lb plates (up to four pairs) LIFTS & ORDER 1. Squat 2. Bench Press 3. Pull-Up (weighted) 4. Overhead Press 5. Barbell Curl 6. Deadlift DAILY STRUCTURE Each lift: - ~5 reps (≈60%) - ~2–3 reps (≈80%) - 1 clean single Loads approximate. Use only 45s and 25s. EXECUTION - All reps controlled - Singles repeatable - No grinding Pressing: shoulders down and back Pull-ups: chest to bar, brief squeeze at top FREQUENCY Daily. BODYWEIGHT Maintain bodyweight ≤186 lbs. Eat to recover, not to excess. BELT Optional. Use only when needed. LOAD REFERENCE Bar = 45 SQUAT Light: 185 = 45 + 45 + 25 Medium: 275 = 45 + 45 + 45 + 25 Heavy: 315 = 45 + 45 + 45 + 45 BENCH Light: 135 = 45 + 45 Medium: 185 = 45 + 45 + 25 Heavy: 225 = 45 + 45 + 45 PULL-UP Light: Bodyweight Medium: +25 Heavy: +45 OVERHEAD PRESS Light: 45 = 45 Medium: 95 = 45 + 25 Heavy: 135 = 45 + 45 CURL Light: 45 = 45 Heavy: 95 = 45 + 25 DEADLIFT Light: 225 = 45 + 45 + 45 Medium: 315 = 45 + 45 + 45 + 45 Heavy: 405 = 45 + 45 + 45 + 45 + 45 STANDARD Did you lift today? If yes, the standard is met. Nothing else counts. No streaks. No credit for past or planned days. END STATE A fixed level of strength, maintained without change, through daily practice.
