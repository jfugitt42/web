The Constitution of Survival

What rules allow many different intelligent civilizations—with incompatible psychologies, values, and histories—to coexist for an extremely long time?

  1. Every civilization has the right to continue existing.
  2. Every civilization has the responsibility not to destroy another.
  3. Agreements require informed consent by all affected parties.
  4. Actions with significant cross-civilization risks require shared oversight.
  5. Knowledge that substantially improves flourishing should be shared unless doing so creates greater danger.
  6. Conflicts should be resolved by agreed processes before force is used.
  7. Diversity of minds, cultures, and forms of life is a resource to preserve, not a defect to eliminate.
  8. Every member is responsible for leaving the interstellar community more stable, more knowledgeable, and more capable of peaceful coexistence than they found it.
